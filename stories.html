The black list

Prologue

The one time that you tried to steal my integrity

I stood alone in my own pretty wooden cabin, and I wondered how it was even possible that there was a cabin like this and no one had considered using it ofr years. Well, I was to make the difference, weren't I?

The phone kept buzzing in my pocket, so I finally resigned and pulled it off. Not a phone call had I received, yet dozens of messages through this one weird app that he had made me install.

The name was simple and not unique: Chat-n-talk. The app was neat and minimalistic, pretty much nothing unexpected to be seen there. It had a few pretty cool features, though. Obviously, you could chat and make phone calls, send short videos, voice mails, emojis and stickers and all of this usual stuff that every messaging app has to have to succeed. What I liked in particular, was how neatly were these features hidden and that you could choose the layout and usage. Apart from this, though, there was this wall, something like Facebook or Instagram, where all the people within the app could post texts (so it might be really more like Twitter rather than anything), but only within certain groups as I understood eventually. It was pretty cool, mostly, thought, I'm afraid just because of the excellent and balanced design of the app. It had light and dark themes and not very much colours into it.

The one group that he had add me to, was the source of all the buzzing. The name was The black list, it was anonymous, and it kept vomitting new posts, each with one name.
